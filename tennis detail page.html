Tennis

Tennis is a popular racket sport played either between two individuals (singles) or two teams of two players each (doubles). It is played on various surfaces such as grass, clay, or hard courts, and it is characterized by its fast-paced nature, strategic gameplay, and emphasis on skill, agility, and endurance.Tennis is a racket sport that can be played individually against a single opponent (singles) or between two teams of two players each (doubles). Each player uses a tennis racket that is strung with cord to strike a hollow rubber ball covered with felt over a net and into the opponent's court Tennis is a game played with two opposing players (singles) or pairs of players (doubles) using tautly strung rackets to hit a ball of specified size, weight, and bounce over a net on a rectangular court.
Equipment:
Racket: Tennis players use a specialized racket with a handle and a frame strung with cords to hit the tennis ball. Tennis Ball: A hollow rubber ball covered with felt, specifically designed for tennis. Court: Tennis is typically played on a rectangular court with a net running across the center, dividing it into two equal halves.
Professional Tennis:
Tournaments: Professional tennis features a wide range of tournaments, including Grand Slam events, ATP Tour, WTA Tour, and ITF (International Tennis Federation) events. Grand Slam Events: The four Grand Slam tournaments are the most prestigious events in tennis, attracting the top players from around the world. They include the Australian Open, French Open (Roland Garros), Wimbledon, and US Open. Rankings and Prizes: Professional players are ranked based on their performance in tournaments, with rankings updated regularly by governing bodies such as the ATP and WTA. Prize money varies depending on the tournament's prestige and is distributed among players based on their performance.

Tennis Rules

1 Equipment:Racket: Players use a racket to hit the ball. Rackets vary in size and weight but must adhere to regulations set by the International Tennis Federation (ITF). Tennis Ball: A tennis ball is a hollow rubber ball covered in felt. It must also meet ITF regulations.
2 Court: Dimensions: The court is 78 feet (23.77 meters) long and 27 feet (8.23 meters) wide for singles matches, and 36 feet (10.97 meters) wide for doubles matches. Lines: The court is divided into various sections by lines. These include the baseline, service line, center service line, center mark, sidelines, and service boxes.

3 Scoring: Game: A game is won by the first player or team to win four points. Points are counted as 15, 30, 40, and then game. If both players or teams reach 40-40, it's called deuce. Deuce: When the score is tied at 40-40, it's called deuce. From deuce, a player must win two consecutive points to win the game. The first point after deuce is called advantage, and if the player wins the next point, they win the game. If the player loses the next point, it's back to deuce. Set: A set is won by the first player or team to win six games, with a margin of at least two games. If the score reaches 6-6, a tiebreak is usually played. Tiebreak: In a tiebreak, the first player or team to reach at least seven points with a margin of at least two points wins the tiebreak and the set. serve each, alternating.
4 Serving:Server: The player who serves is the server, and the player who receives is the receiver.Service Box: The server must stand behind the baseline and hit the ball into the service box diagonally opposite them. Fault: If the server fails to get the ball into the service box, it's called a fault. The server gets a second chance.Double Fault: If the server fails to get the ball into the service box on both attempts, it's called a double fault, and the receiver wins the point.Let: If the ball hits the net and lands in the correct service box on the serve, it's called a let, and the server gets another chance without penalty.
5 Gameplay: Players take turns serving and receiving. The ball must bounce within the opponent's court boundaries for a shot to be considered in-play. Players can hit the ball once it has bounced or before it bounces (volley).A point is won when the opponent fails to return the ball within the boundaries of the court, hits the ball into the net, or commits a double fault.Matches are typically best-of-three sets for women's matches and best-of-five sets for men's matches in major tournaments.
6 Miscellaneous: Players are allowed to request challenges on line calls using Hawk-Eye or similar technology in professional matches. Players are expected to adhere to a code of conduct, which includes good sportsmanship and respect for officials and opponents.
